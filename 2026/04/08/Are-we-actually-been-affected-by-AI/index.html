My confuse about AI back then

A news report gives me more ideas

Today I came across a news report in which an AI expert argued that the current AI economy has a bubble.He also suggested that young people should aquire knowledge across multiple disciplines rather than becoming a specialist in just one area,since you never know which jobs will prove useful in the future.

AI is just an excuse for the layoffs

With all the recent layoffs at big companies,the repoter raised the question of whether this is due to the impact of AI.However,the expert point out that AI might just be an excuse.Because if AI were really the culprit,software engineers would be the first to be affected——their jobs are more exposed to AI.But in reality,they haven’t been hit hard by layoffs,and their salaries haven’t dropped significantly.The real reason for the layoffs is more likely the aftermath of the pandemic:companies hired a lot of people during that time,and now they are left with structural redundancy.
